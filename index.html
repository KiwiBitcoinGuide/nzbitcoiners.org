Why is Bitcoin Important?

Bitcoin is a new kind of digital commodity money launched in 2009. Think of Bitcoin like a digital version of gold. Bitcoin is native to the internet and like gold, does not require anyone’s permission to transact or trade.

The increasing cost of living crisis in New Zealand and the world is due to the broken monetary policy of Governments who have had the ability to freely print money at zero cost. When there is no cost to print money, we get inflation and all parts of society feel the pinch. But when every dollar is backed by something real you are a lot more careful with how you use it.

Bitcoin, though digital, is unique in that it is backed by the energy used by the world’s largest decentralised network of computers. Bitcoin has a fixed supply which means the end of monetary inflation.

What the internet did for the way we interact with information is what bitcoin is doing to the way we think of money.

No banks, no governments, no owner and also no borders. Bitcoin is a global, decentralised and censorship resistant payments network that is already able to be used by merchants, retailers, and individuals.

Beyond a day to day asset and payment tool held by thousands of kiwis, Bitcoin is a positive social success, and is increasingly being used for remittance payments in developing nations, helping smaller nations avoid the debt trap, and to provide a way around financial censorship by governments.
*New Zealand has not had gold-backed money for over a 100 years (RBNZ, Page 45).

FAQ

Do I have to pay tax on Bitcoin income?
For payments Bitcoin is a transfer of money from one form to another, and not an actual purchase that attracts GST. Think of it like a transport card top-up (Snapper, AT Hop, Metrocard, etc)

If you receive Bitcoin as business income then you will have to pay GST and income tax just the same as if you were receiving dollars

See IRD Cryptoassets guidance

Isn't Bitcoin Illegal?
Absolutely not. In fact the NZ government and IRD already permit businesses to pay their staff in Bitcoin and there is clear statements from the Government and the Reserve Bank of New Zealand setting precedent using Bitcoin as a way to pay.

Aren't there many "cryptocurrencies"?
There are a lot several off-shoot digital cash systems, some use the word "Bitcoin" in their names. Bitcoin however is the original, with a perfect launch and no trade offs. Bitcoin is the safest, most used, most decentralised, hard money we've ever had.

What about all these hacks and exchanges going bust?
If you leave your Bitcoin on an exchange or leave it with any 3rd party your funds may be at risk. If you self custody your own Bitcoin in your own Bitcoin wallet your funds are much safer.
